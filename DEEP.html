Key Changes for FY 2024-25

  • New tax regime is now the default option - Taxpayers must explicitly opt for old regime
  • Standard deduction of ₹50,000 introduced in new tax regime
  • Tax exemption limit increased from ₹2.5 lakh to ₹3 lakh under new regime
  • Leave encashment exemption increased from ₹3 lakh to ₹25 lakh for non-government employees
  • LTCG tax exemption remains at ₹1 lakh for listed equity shares/units

Important Deductions & Exemptions (Old Regime Only)

Section 80C Deductions (₹1.5 lakh limit)
  • Public Provident Fund (PPF)
  • Equity Linked Savings Scheme (ELSS)
  • Life Insurance Premiums
  • Employee Provident Fund (EPF)
  • National Savings Certificate (NSC)
  • Principal repayment of home loan
  • Tuition fees for children
Other Key Deductions
  • Section 80D: Health Insurance Premium (₹25,000 for self/family, additional ₹25,000 for parents, ₹50,000 if parents are senior citizens)
  • Section 80TTA: Interest on Savings Account (₹10,000 limit)
  • HRA: House Rent Allowance exemption based on rent paid
  • LTA: Leave Travel Allowance exemption for travel expenses
  • Section 24: Interest on home loan up to ₹2 lakh
